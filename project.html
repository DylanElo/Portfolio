üîç Key Findings (Phase 1: Global Fandom Lens)

Based on MyAnimeList data via Jikan API. Note: MAL has a Western/international bias. Full strategic insights require domestic (Japan) and streaming platform data (see Phase 2 Roadmap).

Question: How do our historical hits compare to new titles?

Finding: Top legacy franchises (Naruto Shippuden: 8.28, Bleach TYBW: 8.99) score competitively with new-wave anime. Legacy IP maintains 2.6M+ MAL members.

Aligns with Netflix data: Naruto Shippuden = #1 anime globally in H1 2025 (40M views).

üìâ New Title Underperformance

Question: Why do newer Pierrot titles struggle?

Finding: Boruto (5.98) and Tokyo Ghoul ‚àöA (7.03) score significantly below competitor benchmarks (JJK: 8.6+, Demon Slayer: 8.7+).

Hypothesis: High filler % + inconsistent production quality damage fan perception.

üåç Global Competition Gap

Question: How do we compare to MAPPA/ufotable?

Context: Jujutsu Kaisen was named "Most in-demand TV series globally" in 2023 at 71.2x average demand (Parrot Analytics). Pierrot's new titles don't match this momentum.

Strategic implication: Quality perception matters more than quantity of episodes.

üí° Strategic Recommendations

Based on Phase 1 analysis + industry benchmarks. Full implementation requires Phase 2 data integration (see Roadmap).

üé¨ Shift to Seasonal Production

Action: Move from long-running formats (Boruto: 293 episodes) to seasonal arcs (JJK: 24 episodes/season). Cap filler at 10% max vs current 40%+.

Why: JJK/Demon Slayer model = higher production quality + better MAL scores.

üì∫ Leverage Legacy IP on Netflix

Action: Prioritize licensing deals for Naruto/Bleach on Netflix (current #1 anime globally). Use revenue to fund high-quality new IP development.

Why: Legacy IP dominates streaming but new IP (Boruto) fails to retain audience.

üåè Compete Globally, Not Just Domestically

Action: Develop 1-2 new "premium quality" IPs targeting international streaming platforms (Netflix, Crunchyroll). Study MAPPA/ufotable production strategies.

Why: Streaming market projected to grow from $3.7B (2023) to $12.5B (2030). Pierrot needs competitive new IP.
